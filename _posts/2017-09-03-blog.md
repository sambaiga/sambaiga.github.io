The energy transition demands innovative solutions, and data-driven approaches are poised to revolutionize low-voltage (LV) power distribution systems. However, researchers face a critical hurdle: limited data availability for developing and validating data analytics and AI-powered solutions. These approaches leverage data analytics and AI to optimize energy generation and consumption, maintaining sustainable energy systems. Harnessing the power of data analytics and machine learning enables organizations and industries to achieve optimal energy generation and consumption, addressing critical challenges in the energy transition. This blog post bridges this gap, empowering researchers like you to delve into the fascinating world of LV distribution systems.

By the end of this post, you will be equipped with the knowledge to understand the process that power engineers use to run a power flow analysis for an LV distribution network and learn how to extract relevant data using [OpenDSS](https://www.epri.com/pages/sa/opendss). OpenDSS is a tool that enables power engineers to simulate and analyze the behaviour of LV distribution systems. By using OpenDSS, researchers can gain insights into the performance and efficiency of LV distribution networks, facilitating the development of innovative solutions for the energy transition.
